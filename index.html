Near-term FCFFs across all companies are too small relative to invested capital to justify current valuations alone. You are buying a decade-long thesis on India's construction future — not next quarter's earnings.

Companies Clearly Earning Above WACC

Only UltraTech's ROIC of 14.2% clearly clears its ~11.7% WACC. Shree is at parity and improving. Dalmia and Ramco are currently destroying equity value with each incremental rupee deployed — a structural issue, not a temporary one.

1.12×

Shree's Sales/Capital — The Sector's Real Moat

Capital efficiency predicts resilience better than margins. Shree needs less new capital per rupee of growth than any competitor — an advantage that compounds dramatically over a decade and is funded from the only net cash balance sheet in the group.

₹1,879

Dalmia's Narrative Risk — Per Share

The gap between Dalmia's base-case intrinsic value (₹222) and its market price (₹2,101) represents the market paying for a future that the base model does not support. This is not necessarily wrong — it is a bet on narrative perfection, made consciously or not.
